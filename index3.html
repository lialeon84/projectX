My Journey

By Rose | June 26

"Engineers like to solve problems. If there are no problems handily available, they will create their own problems." ― Scott Adams

"We are continually faced by great opportunities brilliantly disguised as insoluble problems."  - Lee Iacocca

Since I was a kid, I always had a talent for fixing things. I would fix anything from gadgets to jewelry. At the age of 14, I got my first computer and without any assistance or knowledge I set it up. Installing Google Chrome and connecting to the World Wide Web was the most exciting. I explored my new computer and felt it was my niche. In my early twenties, I disassembled my computer, installed a new hard drive and felt great satisfaction in my work.

When I started working at tech companies, helping customers with their wireless devices, I developed a greater passion. I started exploring free online courses in Web Development. As soon as I started learning basic coding and saw how websites were built, I knew this is where my career path was heading.

"As engineers, we were going to be in a position to change the world - not just study it."  - Henry Petroski

I was waiting for the perfect time to go back to school. Working full-time, it never seemed like it was the right time. I took a leap of faith and enrolled in school for Web Development. It was hard at first, keeping up with work and school I felt like giving up. However, the more I learned and experienced the ability to create websites the more I pushed myself. After I graduated I was promoted to Web Developer at my employer. I give thanks to those who cheered me on and believed in me. I'm proud of myself for taking a leap of faith and believing in myself. As I continue to grow and learn, my only wish is to create amazing things in this world.
